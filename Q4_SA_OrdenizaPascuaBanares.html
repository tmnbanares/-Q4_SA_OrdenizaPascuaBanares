MATH QUIZ

Multiple Choice

Instruction: Read each question carefully and choose the corresponding letter that represents your answer.

1. What is the sum of the greatest 3-digit number and the greatest 4-digit number?






2. On his first birthday, Aster placed one P5 coin in a jar. On his second birthday, he placed two P5 coins in the same jar. Every birthday he had thereafter, he put in as many P5 coins as his age in years. If he now has a total of P140 in the jar,how old is he?






3. A teacher gave a three-item TRUE or FALSE quiz. How many possible combination of answers can students have?






4. A watch is set correctly at 9 A.M. If it loses 2 minutes every hour, what will be the time on my watch if the correct time is 4 P.M. the next day?






5. Thirty-six men ccan finish building a house in 120 days. If a third of the original number of men were added, how many days would it take the whole group to finish the same house?






6. James answered a 40-item quiz. He gets 5 points for every correct answer but he loses 2 points for every wrong answer. What was his total score if he correctly answered 75% of the items?






7. The faces of a big cube were painted. Then it was divided into 27 smaller cubes of the same size. How many small cubes have only one face painted?






8. Eight unit cubes are glued to form a 2 by 2 cube. If one corner unit cube is removed, what is the total surface area of the resulting figure?






9. How many fifty-peso bills are equivalent to 30 twenty-peso bills?






10. An ant walks around the edges of different shapes. In which shape will the ant walk the shortest distance?






11. Mae has a rectangular sheet of paper measuring 48 cm by 72 cm. She wants to cut it into the least number of square pieces without wasting any material. How many squares can she cut from the paper?






12. A rectangle has an area of 100 cm^2. The lengths of its sides are doubled to form a new rectangle. How does the area of the new rectangle compare with the area of the original rectangle?






13. What is the rule for this sequence: -1, 2, 5, 8,..., 26






14. Given the sequence 2,2,3,3 _____. What number could go into the blank?






15. What would be the next term of the sequence: 2, 5, 11, 23, …?
